About Kochi Water Metro

Connecting Islands • Empowering Communities • Greener Transport

Kochi, often referred as the commercial capital of Kerala, is one of the most densely populated districts in the state. The Water Metro project reduces pollution and traffic congestion while improving connectivity between the mainland and island communities.

The project plans 15 routes, 38 terminals, and 78 eco-friendly hybrid electric ferries covering 78 km. Over 33,000 island residents are expected to benefit, making the Water Metro one of India’s most people-focused urban transport initiatives.

It is designed as a socially inclusive transport system, improving livelihoods through tourism, commercial development, and seamless public mobility.

WATERWAYS AND IDENTIFIED ROUTES

The project aims to use the inland waterways in and around Kochi, with major routes spanning National Waterways, Cochin Port Trust Waters, and existing inland channels. The project consists of fifteen (15) identified routes connecting thirty-eight (38) jetties across ten (10) island communities and two boatyards.

The total length of these 15 routes is 76.2 km. Minor dredging is required in selective zones, while the main navigation channels remain largely navigable. The project integrates navigation buoys, night navigation assistance, and environmental measures such as floating waste management.
